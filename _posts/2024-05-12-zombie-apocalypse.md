## Zombie apocalypse

---

![Screen time statistics 2024](/images/screen_time_stats_2024.png)

Yep, this is what happens to the most, if not all, of us. Let's get to some facts:

1. The only valuable thing you do on your phone is texting/talking, which usually is not work related (otherwise you'd be doing it from your laptop probably. RARELY there are emergencies, which, in most cases, are rarely emergencies.)
2. You do not gain anything from watching reels/tiktoks/youtube videos other than short dopamine (i.e. you will not gain anything from it in the future)
3. You are probably addicted and in the back of your head you wish that you weren't

This is the battle we are all fighting right now. Some of us are aware of it, and some of us aren't. This post is written exclusively for people who are aware.

The thing everyone should understand is that you are not battling for life, no. You will be able to live a life regardless whether you waste your time on the phone/in computer games. Yes, you will probably regret doing it at some point, but the funny thing is - nobody cares, sorry. The scariest thing is not that you'll be sad at some point, but that you will stay mediocre. 

George Hotz said on one of his streams that people wasting time on their phones does not mean a new tragic circumstance, but it is rather a transition from one way of wasting time to the other, given technological advancement. I do not fully agree with this statement. 

Back in the day, to get the dopamine (i.e. have some fun) people had to invest much more energy to at least even get to the place (i.e. you had to get up, get on the horse, and ride to the brothel where you can play, drink, laugh, have sex etc.):

![Cowboy](/images/cowboy.png)

Now, all you have to do is open an app on your phone, no need to even get up from a couch. Yes, the amount of dopamine may be smaller, but you don't usually think about it. And the best part - it's affecting your brain biochemistry almost like long-term cocaine usage (I assume having constant dopamine release over prolonged period of time would lead to upregulation of dopamine transporters in presyncaptic cells).

So, as an analogy, modern technology related to entertainment is a simple catalyst in the process of you getting the dopamine. By definition, you require less energy to get the reaction going:

![Catalysts lower the activation energy barrier](/images/catalytic_energy_lowering.png)

I would even define this technology in more narrow definition - it's an enzyme. Enzymes are proteins in our body which play the role of catalysts for different biochemical reactions. And I'd argue that tiktok/instagram are enzymes because they have direct consequences on your body. So, Meta, Google and TikTok are actually producing bioweapons. Yay.

Well, you may have guessed that increasing the rate of chemical reactions sometimes is not great for our health. A lot of enzymes in our body could play a role in diseases pathways, helping cancer to spread or an unwanted bacteria to feed/proliferate. But in the case of instagram and other "entertainment" enzymes you are not able to inhibit them with drugs, unfortunately. 

So, let's get back to mediocrity. You don't need to be a genius to understand that with damaged dopamine mechanism you will not get to the great results. You will still achieve something in your life, but on the deathbed you will have a ton of regrets.

So, how do you fight against the bioweapon being injected into your system? Covid tought us 1 thing - there are no vaccines with KNOWN long-term effects. You can't fight this by "injecting a little bit" and strengthen your system in this case too. You will get sucked up easily as well.

I think the best way to survive this in potential realisation terms is to avoid it. Quarantine yourself and avoid zombies.

Zombies are clearly defined here too - these are "infected" people from your circle. No matter how stoic you are, you value your friends and that's important. Although you should not "ban" them, you need to avoid the infectious bites (people will send you links to youtube videos, memes, reels etc.). Wrap your hands to avoid bites!

![Hands_wrap_against_bites](/images/zombie_bite.jpeg)

In other words, whenever someone sends you the link - tell them directly that you quit and they shouldn't send you stuff like this. In case they don't understand whatever to do next is up to you.

Now, other things are all up to you - change the colors on the phone to black and white to avoid brain contamination with external triggers. Survive 2 weeks without a phone and see where it gets you.

I'm on my way to survive this zombie apocalypse as well.
